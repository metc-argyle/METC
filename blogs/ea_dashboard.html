⚡ Critical Policy Signal
Rising Electric Appliance Imports Signal a Looming Electricity Demand Surge
Nepal's household electric appliance imports have grown 17× by value from 2008/09 to 2024/25, driven by rising incomes, urbanisation, and clean cooking transitions. Refrigerators, washing machines, and air conditioners alone account for nearly NPR 14.7B in 2024/25 imports. The parallel emergence of induction and infrared stoves signals a structural shift from LPG to electric cooking — a trend that will significantly amplify future electricity demand. Nepal's generation and grid infrastructure must anticipate this demand curve today.

The Electric Cooking Revolution
Since 2020/21, Nepal has witnessed a dramatic uptake in electric cooking appliances — induction and infrared stoves — marking a structural shift away from LPG and biomass.
Key Insight
Why electric cooking matters for energy policy
🔌
290,000+ Electric Stoves (2024/25)
Induction and infrared stoves combined reached nearly 290K units imported in 2024/25 — a 35× increase in just 5 years.
📈
India Emerging in Infrared Stoves
In 2025/26, India exported 45,619 infrared stoves to Nepal vs. 13,268 from China — a reversal of the typical China-dominant pattern.
🍚
Rice Cooker Surge
Rice cookers (a staple cooking device) peaked at 442K units in 2020/21 with a value of NPR 449M — almost entirely from China.
⚡
Each Stove = ~0.8–2 kWh/day
At 290K induction stoves operating daily, this adds an estimated 230–580 GWh of additional annual electricity demand.

🔭 Forward-Looking Analysis
Nepal's Appliance Stock Is Building a Silent Demand Surge
The cumulative stock of imported electric appliances — not just annual flows — determines electricity demand. With over 1.3M refrigerators, 500K+ washing machines, 150K+ ACs, and 2M+ electric cooking appliances now in circulation (estimated from import flows since 2008/09), Nepal's household electricity load is structurally higher and will continue to grow with every passing import cycle. Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA) must integrate appliance import data into demand-side planning.

Policy Takeaways
Actionable intelligence for Nepal's policymakers, energy planners, and development stakeholders.
📊
Integrate Appliance Data into IRP
Nepal's Integrated Resource Plan (IRP) should incorporate MoF appliance import statistics as a leading indicator for demand forecasting. The 17× growth in import values since 2008/09 maps directly to future load growth.
🌞
Energy Efficiency Standards Now
With hundreds of thousands of refrigerators and ACs entering Nepal annually, mandatory Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) and star ratings could cut electricity demand growth by 20–30% — critical for grid stability.
🍳
Support the Electric Cooking Transition
The surge in induction stove imports aligns with Nepal's clean cooking targets. Targeted subsidies, rural grid strengthening, and time-of-use tariffs can accelerate adoption while managing grid peaks.
🇨🇳🇮🇳
Diversify Supply Chains
Nepal's electric appliance imports are 92% concentrated in China (36%) and India (56%). Supply chain disruptions — as seen during COVID — can destabilise markets. Diversification of sources and domestic assembly partnerships should be explored.
❄️
Plan for AC-Driven Peak Demand
AC imports have grown 8× since 2017. At ~1.5 kW each, 150K+ cumulative ACs represent 225 MW of potential simultaneous peak load. NEA must plan generation reserves and distribution upgrades accordingly.
📋
Data Collection Improvements
N/A values in early years (especially electric cooking) limit analysis. MoF should standardise appliance-level trade codes and publish granular data earlier in the fiscal year to support real-time policy response.
